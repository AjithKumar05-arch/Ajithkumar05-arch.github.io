Introduction#

I welcome you again in my second reflection blog. In this blog I will be talking about all my learnings and experiences during the Second Unit of the course. The second unit was the last unit before the mid-semester-break. For the First Unit there was not any written exams. But for the second one we had one big exam of 50 marks before we could get the mid-semester-break. So in this blog there is a lot to cover, from learnings to exams. Also, this was the unit where I struggled a lot. So stay tuned and I will take you through all my journey from start to the end of the Second Unit. Let’s begin the journey.

Oh wait! In my first blog, I didn’t mention anything about Lab activities. How can I forget that? It was where we spent more than 70% of the course time. It was most Important part of the course. But no worries, I will tell about everything in this blog. Let’s begin now:

Lab Activities#

I believe that learning comes more from doing things practically than to just knowing about them theoretically. So we had lab activities where we used to apply the concepts learned to test and do things practically. During our First Unit of the course, we did some interesting things with wiki and markdown. Our group was given a chapter(The talking Drum) and prologue from the book “The Information” by ‘James Glieck’ and we had to summarise the chapter using the concepts of wiki. The summarise work we did was on zettlr. Evertime we did something during lab activites, we did that in groups. And from here comes the team work learning. In my past life, I always prefered to work alone. But through all the activities we did in groups during lab work, I learned how important the team work is. Its importance reminds me of one very renowned African Proverb- " If You want to go fast go alone, but If you want to go far go together". I would say this is a great skill that everyone must develop who wants to do something big in life(go far). Then we started doing something on which I am working now and I believe I will continue doing in future as well. We built a Hugo server and conected it to our Github account. This task took a lot of time. It was a lot that many people struggle to complete it till the end of Second Unit.

Second Unit- This was the unit where I struggled a lot(because of maths used). I know that we cannot deny maths if we are Information Science student. Hmm.. But yeah. What can one do if he is weak at something? Struggle! isn’t it? In this unit we mostly played with Images, its building blocks, its transformations, and about every thing that makes up an Image. Yeah that is it ‘Images’. I don’t want to talk much about it. I don’t like it.

What all I learned#

My learnings? I feel I have already disscused it in the Lab activities section. In this section I will talk about life learnings that I experienced during my first and second unit. I repeat again, If you want to go fast, go alone but If you want to go far, go together. This is a great learning that I experienced during my course work. Second thing that never give up on something which you like, where your interests lie, which you can do. Throughout the course work I felt frustrated many times but I kept calm and You see now I know all the things which felt me frustrating earlier. So yeah never give up, things feels harder at begining.

Challenges Faced#

Maths, experiencing new technologies, keeping myself focused those two long our sessions. And I don’t remember much.

Reflection#

Great course for someone who wants to work on ‘IT sector’ or on any sector where there is Data Work.

Conclusion#

To conclude the second unit was both challenging and rewarding. I struggled with maths, new technologies, but remained patient. And worked on the things again and again. I pushed myself out of the comfort zone and tried doing things which scared me the most. And now I know all the things smoothly which felt rough at the begining. I have learned how to use Git hub, Git Bash, Its commands, How to write a blog, how to connect it to github? how to upload it? and a lot of things. And most Importantly, I learned that even if something feels difficult at begining, getting stick to it and keep working on it eventually leads to improvement.

Gratitude#

I am really thankful to our instructor for guiding us through all the way of the first unit. Her teaching pace, friendly nature, and understanding of students has helped me a lot to keep myself engaged in the course. I also pay my sincere thankfulness to my classmates and group members who always stayed by my side when things went difficult and wrong. Last but not the list, I would like to thank myself for never giving up and keeping the spirit up. Thankyou.
