## Introduction

Welcome back to my third reflection blog. In this blog I will talk about my experiences during the third unit of the course- Information Science. The third unit was about Reflecting on failure. To reflect on failure we did some group projects where we dealt with some case studies and chapters from one very good book by Emy Edmondson on failure  "The right kind of wrong: the science of failing well". Actually the group project was that we had to go through the case study articles and chapters from the book and create a wiki and then Individual Viva in front of the class. It was really interesting. I will tell you about everything in my blog ahead. So stay tuned and here we go.


## First Chapter: Group Division

It was the very first day of the class after mid semester break when it all started. It was an evening class. Our class began with playing one game. The whole class was divided into two groups; group 0 and group 1. I was in group 1. Then the 1st group was told to go outside for a 5 minute break. After 5 minutes we were called in and group 2 was sent out. As we got in, anyone from the group was asked to volunteer to spin for the lucky number for the group. I took the opportunity. We got 87 as the lucky number. The number was big. wasn't it?. Then the group was told to fill up one survey form. After the form was filled, group 0 was called in and the comparison of the difference in choices between the two groups were made. The instructor told the class the reason behind the differences in choices. The game was really interesting. Then during the end of the class the whole class was divided into some groups(groups of 4-5 members). Then she introduced the group project on failure and gave the instructions. By the time all this was done, it was already 8:30pm so after this we went for dinner.
